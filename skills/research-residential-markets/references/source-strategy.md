## 1. Source plan

Plan sources by decision question rather than browsing indiscriminately.

| Decision question | Evidence required |
|---|---|
| Is the market deep enough? | households, income, employment, transactions, rental demand |
| Is supply manageable? | completed stock, construction pipeline, approvals, completion timing |
| Which price is achievable? | achieved transactions, net launch prices, incentives, secondary benchmark |
| Which product will sell? | unit mix, size, velocity, remaining inventory, buyer objections |
| How should the project be phased? | recent monthly absorption, market capture, competing completions |
| What could invalidate the strategy? | overhang, cancellations, affordability, financing, planning, pipeline |

For current facts, browse and verify. Prefer primary and authoritative sources. Cite direct supporting pages rather than search results.

## 2. Reliability hierarchy

1. Official statistics, transaction, planning, and regulatory sources.
2. Audited or professionally validated databases.
3. Developer price lists, official brochures, and statutory materials.
4. Timestamped interviews, sales-gallery surveys, and site inspections.
5. Portals, advertisements, and media reports.

Treat portals and media as discovery sources unless corroborated. Treat developer and salesperson performance claims as reported until verified.

## 3. Collection method

For each source:

1. capture the original file, page, URL, or interview record;
2. record source and reference periods separately;
3. extract atomic claims into evidence records;
4. preserve raw wording where the definition is unclear;
5. normalize values only after definitions are reconciled;
6. assign confidence and verification status;
7. link normalized records back to evidence IDs.

Avoid copying a chart value without its definition, geography, period, and source note.

## 4. Primary project surveys

Use a fixed questionnaire and capture:

- units released;
- executed sales;
- bookings or reservations;
- remaining inventory by type;
- launch and survey dates;
- gross and net prices;
- temporary promotions;
- furnishing and legal-fee packages;
- maintenance and sinking fund;
- best- and worst-selling layouts;
- buyer profile and objections;
- rental or return claims;
- price lists, brochures, plans, and geotagged images.

Use disclosed and lawful collection methods. Do not misrepresent an AI system or human researcher. When direct verification is unavailable, label the claim as reported and reduce confidence.

## 5. Geographic adaptation

For a new market, identify equivalents for:

- national and local statistics;
- property transaction registries;
- housing ministries or regulators;
- municipal planning and development approvals;
- transport and infrastructure agencies;
- developer disclosures;
- rental and listing datasets;
- geospatial and travel-time data.

For Kuala Lumpur or Malaysia, examples may include national statistics, official property-market information, local planning materials, developer documents, and primary sales surveys. Verify the current authority, dataset name, coverage, and access terms during each study rather than assuming older references remain current.

## 6. Coverage matrix

Track coverage as complete, partial, unavailable, or not applicable:

| Workstream | Minimum coverage |
|---|---|
| Demand drivers | population or households, income or affordability, employment, accessibility |
| Existing supply | product and geography, preferably historical trend |
| Pipeline | status, units, geography, expected completion, delivery confidence |
| Transactions | volume, value, geography, product, price band, recent period |
| Pricing | achieved secondary and net primary evidence |
| Overhang | geography, product, price or size, official definition |
| Comparables | direct, aspirational, and control projects |
| Performance | launch date, verified sales or clearly labeled bookings, remaining inventory |
| Rental | rent, occupancy, or tenant evidence when investor demand matters |
| Site | planning, accessibility, amenities, and constraints |

Route decisive unavailable evidence to the exception queue. Continue with a disclosed limitation when the missing evidence cannot reasonably change the decision.
