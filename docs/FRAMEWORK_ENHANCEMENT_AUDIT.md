# Framework Enhancement Audit

## Purpose

This audit compares all 61 questions in `FRAMEWORK_ENHANCEMENT_QUESTIONS.md`
against the current seven-stage framework, execution calibration, evidence engine,
and founder answers already preserved in the repository.

It prevents Apex from asking the founder to decide matters that should instead be
resolved through current legal research, authoritative market evidence, or
deterministic engine design.

## Disposition Summary

| Disposition | Count | Meaning |
|---|---:|---|
| Covered | 5 | The existing framework already gives a usable decision rule. |
| Apex work | 37 | Apex should research, calculate, or engineer the answer. Founder input is not required. |
| Founder input | 19 | A lived example, personal boundary, or practitioner judgment is still required. |
| **Total** | **61** | Every enhancement question has one owner. |

`Founder input` does not mean the existing framework is silent. Several items are
partially covered, but the missing part can only be calibrated credibly from real
experience or an explicit founder mandate.

## Deduplicated Founder Questionnaire

Only the following questions should be put to the founder. They consolidate the 19
`Founder input` items above and avoid repeating beliefs already recorded.

### 1. Off-Plan Risk And Cash-Flow Runway

Have you bought, seriously considered, or rejected an off-plan property? Describe
how much construction-period payment you would tolerate without rental income, the
cash reserve you would require, and which investor profiles you believe should not
take that risk.

Prefilled context: you trust proven master developers more than pocket-land or
inexperienced developers, and you accept that promised quality cannot be verified
fully before vacant possession.

### 2. Auction Inspection And Hidden-Defect Boundary

Have you bought or seriously attempted an auction purchase? Explain what you would
do when the interior cannot be inspected and how you distinguish a valuable low
reserve price from a title, access, condition, or possession problem.

Prefilled context: auction results are strong price evidence, but a cheap property
must still be valuable rather than merely distressed.

### 3. Auction Concentration Threshold

Name any real project where repeated auctions changed your view. Approximately how
many cases, over what period, project size, and purchase cohort would turn isolated
owner distress into a project-level warning or rejection?

Prefilled context: one auction may be personal distress; many auctions can reveal
bulk-purchase concentration and weak holding power.

### 4. Rental Tax And Ownership Structure

How do you handle rental-income tax in practice, which deductions do you claim, and
has tax ever changed a hold, sell, or refinance decision? Have you compared owning
personally with joint or company ownership? Record no direct experience where that
is the honest answer.

### 5. Flexi Loans And Lock-In Experience

Have you used a full-flexi or semi-flexi loan, refinanced during a lock-in period, or
paid an early-exit penalty? Give the actual benefit, cost, liquidity effect, and the
rule Apex should remember from that case.

Prefilled context: interest rate alone is not enough; leverage is acceptable only
when the asset performs and the borrower survives the downside.

### 6. Joint Borrowing And Co-Ownership

Have you used or observed joint borrowing or joint title with a spouse, family
member, or partner? What improved approval or affordability, what created conflict
or exit difficulty, and when should Apex discourage it?

### 7. MRTA, MLTA, And Coverage Lessons

Which protection do you use or prefer, MRTA or MLTA, and why? Describe any claim,
refinancing issue, financed premium, coverage gap, or real case that should change
the all-in-cost or risk assessment.

### 8. Flood, Water, Or Slope Site Evidence

Describe a development where flood history, basement water, drainage, slope, or
landslide risk affected your decision. Which physical signs or management answers
were decisive, and which condition should be a hard stop rather than a scored risk?

Prefilled context: safety and flood stigma are difficult to reverse, and site
observation can reveal conditions that market data misses.

### 9. Sinking Fund And Special-Levy Reality

Give one real example of a financially healthy or unhealthy JMB or management
corporation. What were the outstanding-fee level, sinking-fund condition, major
upcoming repair, and any special levy? Which evidence should block a purchase?

Prefilled context: fast, solution-oriented management is positive; poor response,
self-interest, disputes, and weak maintenance are serious warnings.

### 10. Tenant Failure And Recovery

Describe your worst tenant failure or the closest case you directly observed. How
long did recovery or replacement take, what did it cost, and which screening check,
inspection routine, deposit, or agreement term would have prevented the damage?

### 11. Room Letting And Outsourced Management

Have you operated rooms separately, rented a whole unit, or hired a property
manager? Compare achieved rent, vacancy, tenant turnover, wear, management effort,
fees, and the warning signs that made the arrangement succeed or fail.

### 12. Real Disposal Execution

Describe one completed or seriously attempted resale: how you selected agents,
whether the listing was exclusive or open, whether the unit was vacant or tenanted,
what you spent on staging or light renovation, and when you reduced or refused to
reduce the price. State which action measurably improved or damaged the result.

Prefilled context: the preferred exit is normally renovated, staged, and vacant for
owner-occupiers, while a strong tenancy can support an investor exit.

## Processing Rule

1. Founder answers are evidence, not automatic universal rules.
2. Separate a personal preference from a repeatable market mechanism.
3. Record real examples with project, date, price band, and outcome where possible.
4. Convert stable answers into a hard stop, blocker, scored criterion, confidence
   cap, stress assumption, or advisory note.
5. Let Apex research current law, tax, policy, and market facts independently.
6. Preserve uncertainty when the founder has no direct experience.
